General industrial biotechnology and innovative approaches in bioeconomics

Ulubieva Elena1,*,   Ayna Salamova2

1North Ossetian State University, Vladikavkaz, Russia

2Chechen State University, 32 Sheripova str., Grozny, 367024, Russia

Abstract

In the first and, especially, in the second decade of the 21st century, many different biotechnologies from scientific developments began to be transformed and scaled up for industrial use, which was reflected in the relevant publications. Thus, in the ScienceDirect database for 2001, about 1500 articles with the keywords “industrial biotechnology” were annotated, in 2010 there were already almost 3500 of them, and in 2020 - more than 10000. Since the promoted biotechnologies were focused on different sectors of industry and other activities, there were proposals to carry out the systematization of biotechnologies with this in mind. This special color coding is sometimes referred to as The “Rainbow” methodology
